Introduction

A DC regulated power supply is an essential component in electronic circuits, providing a stable and consistent DC voltage. It converts an unstable AC voltage into a steady DC voltage suitable for powering electronic devices.

Block Diagram of a DC Regulated Power Supply

Explanation of Each Block

  • Transformer: Steps down the AC voltage to a lower level suitable for rectification.
  • Rectifier: Converts the AC voltage into pulsating DC voltage.
  • Filter: Smooths out the rectified DC voltage by removing ripples.
  • Voltage Regulator: Maintains a constant DC output voltage regardless of input or load variations.
  • Load: The device or circuit that uses the regulated DC voltage.

Practice Problems

Below are ten practice problems with corresponding circuit diagrams. Solve each problem by analyzing the circuit and determining the requested parameters.

  • 1. Calculate the percentage voltage regulation of a power supply with 500V no-load and 400V full-load output.
  • 2. If a power supply has a voltage regulation of 2% and a no-load output voltage of 50V, determine the full-load voltage.
  • 3. Compare two power supplies based on their voltage regulation characteristics. • Power Supply X has a no-load voltage of 60V and a full-load voltage of 55V. • Power Supply Y has a no-load voltage of 60V and a full-load voltage of 59V. Which power supply has better voltage regulation?
  • 4. When a 2A load is added to a 3A load, the output voltage of the power supply drops from 15V to 14.5V. (i) Calculate the output impedance of the power supply. (ii) Determine the no-load voltage of the power supply.
  • 5. A DC power supply is delivering 12V to a load that is varying sinusoidally between 1A and 2A at a frequency of 15 kHz. The output impedance of the power supply is 0.02Ω at 15 kHz. Calculate the fluctuations in the output voltage caused by this periodic load change.
  • 6. If the no-load output voltage of a DC power supply is 200V and the output voltage decreases to 180V when a full load of 10A is applied, calculate the voltage regulation of the power supply.
  • 7. A power supply has a voltage regulation of 3%. If the no-load voltage is 25V, calculate the voltage under full load.
  • 8. The output voltage of a DC power supply decreases from 18V to 17.5V as a load current increases from 4A to 5A. Calculate the output impedance of the power supply.
  • 9. Two DC power supplies, P1 and P2, have the following characteristics: • P1: No-load voltage = 50V, Full-load voltage = 48V. • P2: No-load voltage = 50V, Full-load voltage = 49V. Which power supply is better, and why?
  • 10. A DC power supply is providing a constant output of 24V to a load that varies sinusoidally between 0.8A and 1.5A at a frequency of 20 kHz. The output impedance of the power supply is 0.05Ω at 20 kHz. Calculate the fluctuations in the output voltage caused by this periodic load change.

Summary

A DC regulated power supply ensures a stable voltage output for electronic circuits by converting AC to DC and regulating fluctuations. It consists of key components such as a transformer, rectifier, filter, and voltage regulator. The transformer steps down AC voltage, the rectifier converts it to DC, the filter smooths out fluctuations, and the voltage regulator maintains a steady output. This type of power supply is widely used in powering microcontrollers, testing circuits, charging batteries, and industrial automation. Understanding its function and troubleshooting common issues—such as incorrect voltage output or overheating components—is essential for electronics applications.
